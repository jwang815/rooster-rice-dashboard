Key Takeaways

What's Going Well

  • Massive profitability turnaround: Swung from a −$15.1K loss in P2 2025 to +$23.6K profit — a $38.7K improvement.
  • Delivery is the growth engine: Delivery sales up $49.7K (+23.7% YoY) to $259K, now 52% of total food sales.
  • 3rd-party delivery fees slashed 64%: Down from $60.1K → $38.4K, saving $21.7K. Likely renegotiated or shifted to direct ordering.
  • 3rd-party discounts nearly eliminated: $64 vs $6,772 PY — stopped subsidizing marketplace promos.
  • Catering growing fast: Up 37.5% YoY ($49.7K vs $36.1K). Santa Clara alone drove $27K.
  • Guest traffic surging: +19.5% more guests (17,612 vs 14,744). Brand awareness clearly building.
  • Santa Clara efficiency: Best net margin at 8.4% with lowest labor % (27.6%). Well-run unit.
  • Prime cost improving: 52.8% vs 54.6% PY — healthy direction for a fast-casual concept.

Areas Needing Attention

  • Stonestown is underwater: Only location posting a loss (−$741). Occupancy at 16.7% of sales is crushing — rent + CAM + property tax = $13.9K/period. Marketing discounts of $3.1K (3.7% of sales) aren't translating to profitability.
  • Marketing spend 8× higher: Advertising jumped from $2.7K to $21.6K. Needs ROI analysis — is it driving the guest count lift, or is it overspend?
  • Poultry costs rising: Up $14.4K (+31%) to $60.9K. As the core protein, this erodes margin if not addressed via supplier negotiation or menu pricing.
  • Produce costs up 82%: $17.5K vs $9.6K PY. Partially volume-driven, but the % of sales also rose (3.6% vs 2.3%).
  • Dinner daypart declining: Dinner sales down $11.6K (−16.2%). Lunch and delivery are cannibalizing the dine-in evening crowd.
  • Average check slipping: $28.21 vs $28.59 PY. Guest growth is outpacing revenue growth per visit — consider menu price optimization.
  • Insurance costs doubling: Business insurance + workers' comp up significantly. Worth reviewing policy terms.
  • Vacation/sick pay jump: $5.9K vs $1.1K PY. May be a catch-up accrual — confirm one-time vs recurring.

Bottom Line for Investors

Rooster & Rice is in a much stronger position than a year ago. The business has successfully pivoted toward a delivery-heavy model, renegotiated (or optimized) third-party delivery economics, and grown guest traffic by nearly 20%. Five of six locations are profitable, and the overall 4.7% net margin is respectable for a fast-casual restaurant chain in high-cost Bay Area markets.

The near-term priorities should be: (1) decide on Stonestown — renegotiate the lease or consider closure, as occupancy alone eats 16.7% of sales; (2) audit the $21.6K marketing spend for ROI; (3) manage food cost inflation, especially poultry, through supplier diversification or modest price increases; and (4) reinvigorate the dinner daypart or lean further into the delivery/catering model that's clearly working.
